NTC Toledano — CNN + Transformer Hybrid (end-to-end trained)
Model: learned nt embedding (dim=32) → 2x Conv1D (64ch, k=5,3) → 2x Transformer (d=64, h=4, ffn=128) → attention pool → MLP (64→32→1)
Input: ±10 nt window around stop codon = 23 nt total
Training: AdamW lr=1e-3, wd=1e-4, Huber loss, batch=512, early stopping patience=20, grad clip=1.0
CV: 5-fold KFold, seed=42, target standardised per fold (train only)
GPU: H100 80GB HBM3
Runtime: ~15 min total for all 5 drugs

Key takeaways:
- End-to-end training on 23 nt window massively outperforms frozen 500M-param NT-v2-500M embeddings
- DAP is most predictable (R²=0.80): local sequence context around stop codon is highly informative
- SJ6986 is hardest (R²=0.44), suggesting additional factors beyond local sequence
- Very low variance across folds (e.g. DAP ±0.004), indicating stable training
- Next steps: try larger context windows (±20, ±63 nt) to see if more context helps
